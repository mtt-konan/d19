6.2 Qualities of the algorithm
Summarizing the observations made in section 4, we see that a search within the
algorithm in which the parameters╧Б0 and ╧Б1 of the central loop are considered up to
logarithmic height n, the solutions will have logarithmic height of about12n. Hence a
search with parameters up to around10 000 is expected to ямБnd solutions with about

a few minutes. However, as mentioned in section 4, the complexity of the algorithm
grows quadratically with the parameters(╧Б0, ╧Б1). Hence a search in a parameter range
of up to 100 000, which could provide solution with about70 decimal places, would
already take several hours.
In spite of this complexity defect, the examples of section 5 show that we can

solutions found by the algorithm, depending on the coeямГcients of the original elliptic
curves. Of course the data are too sparse to lead to serious conjectures. Nevertheless,
the diagrams in section 5 suggest that for the families considered there may be a linear
correlation between the parameters deямБning the curves and the logarithmic height of
the smallest solutions. We are far from being able to formulate such a suspected
correlation in more precise terms.
6.3 Structural questions
The central point in both the weak as well as the strong version of the algorithm is the
consideration of a suitable homogeneous spaceQ over the given elliptic curveE. Such
a homogeneous space is geometrically (i.e. over an algebraic closure ofQ) isomorphic

rational point, and in this situation it is isomorphic overQ to some rationally deямБned
elliptic curve EQ. Via the well known construction of Nagell (cf. [15]) we can deямБne a
biregular mapping fromQ to EQ given by a Weierstra├Я equation.

on E by considering the composition of an isomorphism ofQ to EQ and the mapping

6.4 Further outlook
The algorithm above was developed for the class of elliptic curvesE with full 2-torsion,
which means that the torsion subgroup ofE contains Z/2Z├Ч Z/2Z. Equivalently, the
curves considered can be given in aямГne form by a Weierstra├Я equation with split poly-

could try to develop an algorithm over such a number ямБeld and then,a fortiori, try
